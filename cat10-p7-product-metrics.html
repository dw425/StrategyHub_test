North Star Metric

The single metric that captures your product's core value

‚≠ê

2.4M
Weekly Active Users

What Makes a Good North Star?

Value-Focused: Reflects the core value users get from your product. Not revenue‚Äîthat's an output.

Actionable: Teams can influence it through their work. Not external factors you can't control.

Leading: Predicts future success. Early warning when things go wrong.

Examples by Company Type

  • Spotify: Time spent listening (engagement)
  • Airbnb: Nights booked (transactions)
  • Slack: Messages sent (adoption)
  • Facebook: Daily active users (habitual usage)
  • Shopify: GMV processed (merchant success)
  • Zoom: Meeting minutes (core value delivered)

Why DORA Matters

Research shows elite performers ship 208x more frequently than low performers while maintaining higher quality. Speed and stability aren't tradeoffs‚Äîthey reinforce each other.

Performance Levels

Elite: Multiple deploys/day, <1hr lead time, <1hr recovery, <15% failure rate.

Low: Monthly deploys, 1-6mo lead time, 1wk-1mo recovery, 46-60% failure rate.

Metrics Best Practices

Guidelines for effective product measurement

‚úì Do

‚úì Pick one North Star metric that reflects core value
‚úì Measure outcomes, not just outputs
‚úì Use cohort analysis for retention metrics
‚úì A/B test before making major changes
‚úì Segment metrics by user type and behavior
‚úì Review metrics weekly with the team
‚úì Set leading indicators, not just lagging

‚úó Don't

‚úó Track too many metrics‚Äîfocus on 5-7 key ones
‚úó Use vanity metrics that don't drive decisions
‚úó Ignore statistical significance in experiments
‚úó Optimize one metric at the expense of others
‚úó Compare metrics without context or segments
‚úó Set metrics and forget them‚Äîreview regularly
‚úó Let metrics become targets (Goodhart's Law)
