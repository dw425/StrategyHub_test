Launch Countdown Framework

Structured timeline from T-8 weeks to Go-Live

Go-live planning follows a countdown structure with specific milestones and gates. Each phase has defined deliverables, stakeholder checkpoints, and exit criteria. Missing a milestone should trigger escalation‚Äînot schedule compression. The countdown provides visibility and accountability across all workstreams.

‚è±Ô∏è Go-Live Countdown Timeline
8-Week Runway
T-8 Weeks: Planning Kickoff
Complete scope, team, timeline
Finalize go-live scope and exclusions. Assemble implementation team with clear roles. Create master schedule with all workstreams. Identify critical path and dependencies. Establish communication cadence.
T-6 Weeks: Environment Ready
Infrastructure and data prepared
Production environment provisioned and hardened. Data migration complete with validation. Integrations tested end-to-end. Performance baseline established. Security scans passed.
T-4 Weeks: UAT Begins
User acceptance testing starts
Business users execute test scripts. Defects logged, triaged, and prioritized. Training materials finalized. Change communications drafted. Support team training begins.
T-2 Weeks: Dress Rehearsal
Full cutover simulation
Execute complete cutover runbook with timing. Validate rollback procedures. Test monitoring and alerting. Confirm war room logistics. All critical defects resolved.
T-1 Week: Go/No-Go Decision
Final readiness checkpoint
Formal Go/No-Go meeting with all stakeholders. All readiness criteria evaluated. Executive sign-off obtained. Final communications sent. Support team on standby.
T-48 Hours: Code Freeze
No changes permitted
Absolute code freeze‚Äîno exceptions without executive approval. Final system health checks. Team briefings complete. On-call schedules confirmed. War room activated.

Comprehensive assessment across all go-live factors

Go-live readiness isn't just about technology. Successful launches require alignment across five dimensions: Technical, Business, Operational, Organizational, and Communication. Weakness in any dimension can derail an otherwise well-planned deployment. Each dimension has specific criteria, owners, and acceptance thresholds.

Go/No-Go Decision Framework

Structured process for the final launch decision

The Go/No-Go meeting is the formal decision point where stakeholders collectively decide whether to proceed with go-live. The decision should be based on objective criteria, not optimism. A structured framework removes emotion and ensures consistency across launches.

üö¶ Go/No-Go Decision Tree
Decision Logic
Agenda Item Duration Owner Outcome
1. Technical Readiness Review 15 min Tech Lead Confirm all technical criteria met
2. Business Readiness Review 15 min Product Owner Confirm UAT sign-off and training complete
3. Operational Readiness Review 10 min Ops Manager Confirm support model ready
4. Risk Review 15 min Project Manager Review open risks and mitigations
5. Go/No-Go Vote 5 min Sponsor Formal decision recorded
6. Next Steps 5 min Project Manager Cutover timing confirmed or reschedule

Pre-Launch Risk Assessment

Identify, assess, and mitigate go-live risks

Every go-live carries risk. The goal isn't to eliminate all risk‚Äîit's to understand, accept, and mitigate risks to acceptable levels. A formal risk assessment ensures stakeholders make informed decisions about proceeding with launch.

Risk Category Likelihood Impact Mitigation Owner
SSO integration timeout under load Technical Medium High Increased timeout thresholds, fallback auth option DevOps
Data migration discrepancies Technical Low Critical Reconciliation reports, manual correction procedures Data Team
User adoption below target Business Medium Medium Champions network, enhanced support, incentives Change Lead
Support queue overwhelmed Operational High Medium 3x staffing week 1, self-service KB, chatbot Support Mgr
Third-party vendor unavailable External Low High Confirmed support window, backup contacts Vendor Mgr
Executive sponsor unavailable Organizational Low Medium Delegate authority documented, backup decision-maker PM
‚úÖ Go-Live Planning Best Practices
  • Start early ‚Äî 8+ weeks runway prevents compressed timelines and shortcuts
  • Use objective criteria ‚Äî Emotion-free decisions based on measurable outcomes
  • Rehearse completely ‚Äî Full dress rehearsal with timing, not just task completion
  • Document everything ‚Äî Evidence for every checklist item, not assumptions
  • Plan for failure ‚Äî Rollback is a valid outcome, not a defeat
  • Over-communicate ‚Äî Stakeholders should never be surprised by go-live status
‚ùå Common Go-Live Planning Mistakes
  • Optimism bias ‚Äî "It'll be fine" is not a risk mitigation strategy
  • Skipping rehearsals ‚Äî First-time execution during go-live always fails
  • Incomplete checklists ‚Äî Unchecked items assumed complete
  • Single decision-maker ‚Äî Go/No-Go should be collective, not autocratic
  • Ignoring warnings ‚Äî Yellow flags become red flags during cutover
  • Compressed timelines ‚Äî Skipping phases to meet arbitrary deadlines

Stakeholder Sign-Off Matrix

Who must approve before go-live proceeds

Go-live requires explicit sign-off from key stakeholders. This isn't bureaucracy‚Äîit ensures shared ownership of the decision and commitment to support the launch. Without formal sign-off, accountability is unclear when issues arise.

üìù Sign-Off Documentation
Each sign-off should be formally documented with: (1) Stakeholder name and role, (2) Date and time of sign-off, (3) Scope of approval, (4) Any conditions or caveats, (5) Evidence reviewed. Email confirmation is minimum; digital signature preferred for audit trail.

# Go-Live Readiness Assessment Agent
from crewai import Agent, Task, Crew
from langchain_anthropic import ChatAnthropic

llm = ChatAnthropic(model="claude-sonnet-4-20250514")

# Define Readiness Assessment Agent
readiness_agent = Agent(
    role="Go-Live Readiness Assessor",
    goal="Evaluate all readiness dimensions and provide Go/No-Go recommendation",
    backstory="""Expert in implementation readiness with 
    deep experience across technical, business, and 
    operational dimensions. Data-driven assessor who 
    identifies gaps before they become blockers.""",
    llm=llm,
    tools=[
        checklist_verifier,
        risk_analyzer,
        stakeholder_tracker,
        timeline_monitor
    ]
)

# Readiness Assessment Task
assessment_task = Task(
    description="""
    Assess go-live readiness across all dimensions:
    1. Verify all checklist items with evidence
    2. Calculate readiness scores per dimension
    3. Identify gaps and blockers
    4. Evaluate risk profile
    5. Check stakeholder sign-off status
    6. Provide Go/No-Go recommendation with confidence
    """,
    agent=readiness_agent,
    expected_output="Readiness report with scores, gaps, risks, and recommendation"
)

def assess_readiness(project_id: str) -> dict:
    """Run full readiness assessment for a project."""
    crew = Crew(
        agents=[readiness_agent],
        tasks=[assessment_task],
        verbose=True
    )
    result = crew.kickoff(inputs={"project_id": project_id})
    return {
        "recommendation": result.recommendation,
        "confidence": result.confidence,
        "scores": result.dimension_scores,
        "blockers": result.blockers,
        "risks": result.risks
    }
