Test Pyramid

Layered validation strategy from unit to UAT

The test pyramid ensures comprehensive coverage at every level. Unit tests validate individual SQL statements and functions, integration tests verify object interactions, system tests confirm end-to-end workflows, and UAT validates business acceptance criteria with real users.

Each layer catches different types of defects. Unit tests are fast and numerous, catching syntax and logic errors early. Higher layers are slower but validate complex interactions and business requirements.

Data Comparison

Row-level validation between source and target

Data comparison validates that the migrated data exactly matches the source. We compare row counts, column checksums, and sample record contents across all critical tables. Any discrepancy triggers immediate investigation.

For large tables, we use statistical sampling combined with checksum validation to verify data integrity without comparing every row. Critical tables are validated at 100% coverage regardless of size.

Specialized test suites by validation type

Each category has its own entry/exit criteria, with critical categories blocking deployment if any tests fail. Non-critical categories may proceed with documented exceptions and remediation plans.

Reconciliation runs continuously during migration and post-cutover. For large tables, we use statistical sampling combined with checksum validation. Critical tables are validated at 100% coverage regardless of size.

Defect Tracking

Open issues from validation testing

Defects discovered during validation are tracked through resolution. Each defect is assigned a severity based on business impact, an owner responsible for resolution, and a target date aligned with wave deployment schedules.

Critical and high-severity defects block wave deployment. Medium and low defects may be deferred with documented business justification and remediation timeline.
