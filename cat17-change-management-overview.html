The ADKAR Model

Prosci's individual change management framework

ADKAR is a goal-oriented change management model that guides individual and organizational change. It focuses on achieving five key outcomes that must occur in sequence for change to be successful. Each building block must be substantially complete before moving to the next.

The model is particularly powerful because it provides a diagnostic tool to identify where individuals are stuck in the change process, enabling targeted interventions rather than generic change activities.

üîÑ ADKAR Change Model
PROSCI
A
Awareness
Understanding why the change is necessary. Communicate the business reasons, risk of not changing, and what's in it for them.
D
Desire
Personal choice to support and participate. Address WIIFM (What's In It For Me), engage sponsors, and manage resistance.
K
Knowledge
Understanding how to change. Provide training, education, and information on new processes, tools, and behaviors.
A
Ability
Demonstrated capability to implement. Practice, coaching, hands-on support, and time to develop new skills and behaviors.
R
Reinforcement
Sustaining the change. Recognition, rewards, feedback, accountability, and corrective actions to prevent regression.

Key Concepts

Core principles of effective change management

üëî
Executive Sponsorship
Active and visible executive sponsor is the #1 contributor to change success. Sponsors authorize, legitimize, and demonstrate commitment to change.
üë•
Stakeholder Analysis
Systematic identification and assessment of all parties impacted by change. Maps influence, impact, and sentiment to prioritize engagement.
üì¢
Communication Planning
Structured approach to delivering the right message, to the right audience, through the right channel, at the right time. Address awareness and desire.
üéì
Training & Coaching
Building knowledge and ability through formal training, on-the-job learning, and coaching support. Different roles need different training approaches.

Resistance Management

Adoption Metrics
Measuring change progress through leading indicators (training completion, engagement) and lagging indicators (usage, proficiency, outcomes).
