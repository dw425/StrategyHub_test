Hypercare Support Model

Tiered support structure for post-go-live period

Hypercare is the intensive support period immediately following go-live. It's characterized by elevated staffing, accelerated SLAs, and proactive monitoring. The goal is to rapidly identify and resolve issues before they impact user confidence and adoption. Hypercare typically lasts 2-6 weeks, with intensity decreasing as the system stabilizes.

üéØ Triage Decision Rules
When in doubt, escalate up. It's better to over-prioritize and de-escalate than to under-prioritize and miss a critical issue. During hypercare Week 1, default to one priority level higher than normal. If multiple P1s occur simultaneously, triage by user count affected, then by revenue impact.

Escalation Paths

When and how to escalate issues

Clear escalation paths ensure issues reach the right decision-makers at the right time. Escalation is not failure‚Äîit's good judgment. The escalation path is based on time (SLA breach risk), impact (scope expansion), or complexity (need for senior expertise).

Hypercare Staffing Model

Resource allocation across the hypercare period

Hypercare staffing follows a front-loaded model‚Äîmaximum resources at go-live, gradually tapering to BAU levels. Week 1 typically requires 3x normal staffing with 24/7 coverage. By week 4-6, staffing approaches normal levels with extended hours only.

Shift Handoff
15-minute overlap between shifts for context transfer. Open tickets, pending issues, and hot topics must be handed off explicitly‚Äînot assumed.
üìû
On-Call Rules
On-call must respond within 15 minutes. No alcohol, must have laptop and connectivity. Backup on-call if primary unavailable.

No more than 12-hour shifts. Minimum 8 hours between shifts. Rotate high-stress P1 work across team members.

Hypercare Exit Criteria

When to transition to BAU support

Exiting hypercare too early risks user confidence; too late wastes resources. Exit criteria should be objective and measurable. All criteria must be met for minimum 5 consecutive business days before exit is approved.

‚úÖ Hypercare Best Practices
  • Daily standups ‚Äî 15-min check-in on tickets, trends, and blockers
  • Proactive outreach ‚Äî Check in with key users, don't wait for complaints
  • Document everything ‚Äî Every issue becomes a KB article
  • Celebrate wins ‚Äî Acknowledge team effort and success milestones

‚ùå Common Hypercare Mistakes
  • Understaffing Week 1 ‚Äî Volume always exceeds predictions
  • No escalation paths ‚Äî Issues languish without clear ownership
  • Premature exit ‚Äî Leaving before stability is proven
  • Hero culture ‚Äî One person handling everything unsustainably
  • Ignoring patterns ‚Äî Fixing symptoms, not root causes
  • Poor handoff ‚Äî BAU team unprepared to take over

Knowledge Base Strategy

Building self-service resources during hypercare

Every support ticket is an opportunity to build your knowledge base. A robust KB reduces ticket volume by 30-50% and improves resolution times. During hypercare, prioritize creating articles for the most common and impactful issues.

Article Type Trigger to Create Content Elements Priority
üîß How-To Guide 3+ tickets asking same "how do I..." Step-by-step instructions, screenshots, video if complex High
üêõ Known Issue Bug identified with workaround Symptoms, workaround steps, expected fix timeline High
‚ùì FAQ Repeated questions in training/support Question, concise answer, links to detailed guides Medium
üîó Integration Guide Connectivity or sync questions Setup steps, troubleshooting, contact for issues Medium
üìã Best Practice Users doing things inefficiently Recommended approach, why it's better, examples Lower
üé• Video Tutorial Complex workflow, visual process Screen recording, narration, chapter markers Lower
üìù
Article Template
Standardize format: Title, Summary, Applies To, Steps, Screenshots, Related Articles, Last Updated. Consistency improves findability.
üîç
Search Optimization
Include common misspellings, synonyms, and error messages in metadata. Users search how they think, not how you write.
üìä
Usage Analytics
Track article views, search terms with no results, and "was this helpful" ratings. Data drives KB improvement priorities.
üéØ KB Coverage Target
By end of hypercare, your KB should cover 80% of Tier 1 ticket types. Track "deflection rate" (users who viewed KB and didn't submit ticket). Target: 40% deflection rate within 4 weeks. Every 10% improvement in deflection = significant support cost reduction.

User Feedback Collection

Capturing sentiment and improvement opportunities

Support tickets only capture problems‚Äîthey miss silent frustration and unspoken praise. Proactive feedback collection provides a complete picture of user experience and identifies improvement opportunities before they become complaints.

Method 1
In-App Surveys
Triggered after key actions (first login, complete workflow)
Keep to 1-3 questions maximum
NPS: "How likely to recommend?" (0-10)
CES: "How easy was this?" (1-5)
Open text: "What would you improve?"
Response rate: 15-25%
Method 2
Post-Ticket Survey
Sent after ticket resolution
CSAT: "How satisfied with support?" (1-5)
Resolution: "Was your issue resolved?" (Y/N)
Effort: "How easy to get help?" (1-5)
Follow-up on low scores within 24 hours
Response rate: 20-35%
Method 3
Champion Check-Ins
Weekly 15-min calls with power users
Qualitative feedback on experience
Early warning on emerging issues
Feature requests and enhancement ideas
Builds advocacy and relationship
Insight quality: Highest

# Hypercare Support Assistant Agent
from crewai import Agent, Task, Crew
from langchain_anthropic import ChatAnthropic

llm = ChatAnthropic(model="claude-sonnet-4-20250514")

# Define Hypercare Support Agent
hypercare_agent = Agent(
    role="Hypercare Support Assistant",
    goal="Maximize issue resolution speed and user satisfaction",
    backstory="""Expert support coordinator with deep 
    knowledge of triage, escalation, and rapid resolution. 
    Combines technical troubleshooting with empathetic 
    user communication.""",
    llm=llm,
    tools=[
        ticket_triage_tool,
        kb_search_tool,
        sla_monitor,
        escalation_predictor,
        similar_issue_finder
    ]
)

# Ticket triage and routing
async def triage_ticket(ticket: dict) -> dict:
    """Intelligently triage and route incoming ticket."""
    task = Task(
        description=f"""
        Analyze ticket and determine:
        1. Priority (P1-P4) based on impact/urgency
        2. Category and subcategory
        3. Appropriate support tier
        4. Similar resolved tickets for reference
        5. Suggested resolution steps
        6. SLA timeline based on priority
        
        Ticket: {ticket}
        """,
        agent=hypercare_agent,
        expected_output="Triage decision with routing and suggestions"
    )
    
    crew = Crew(agents=[hypercare_agent], tasks=[task])
    result = await crew.kickoff_async()
    
    return {
        "priority": result.priority,
        "tier": result.assigned_tier,
        "similar_tickets": result.similar_issues,
        "suggested_resolution": result.resolution_steps,
        "sla_deadline": result.sla_time
    }
