Cutover Overview

Orchestrated transition from legacy to modern platform

Cutover is the carefully orchestrated transition from the source data platform to the target Databricks environment. This is the culmination of weeks of assessment, conversion, and validation workâ€”the moment when the new platform goes live and business operations switch over.

Success requires meticulous planning, clear communication, and extensively tested rollback procedures. Every step is documented in the runbook, and a dedicated war room monitors execution in real-time. The goal is zero unplanned downtime and seamless business continuity.

The cutover follows a blue-green deployment strategy: the target environment runs in parallel during final sync, then traffic is switched atomically. If issues arise, we can instantly revert to the source system within the rollback window.

Go/No-Go Decision

Criteria evaluation for cutover approval

The go/no-go decision is made at T-0 (cutover start time) based on objective criteria. All "Go" criteria must be met to proceed. If any single "No-Go" condition exists, cutover is postponed to the next available window (typically 1 week later).

The Cutover Lead has final authority but must document any decision to override criteria with explicit risk acceptance. Escalation to the Steering Committee is required if there's disagreement among leads or if proceeding with known risks.

Execution Runbook

Step-by-step commands with real-time status

The runbook provides exact commands and verification steps for each phase of cutover. Every step has an assigned owner, expected duration, and explicit success criteria. The runbook is version-controlled and requires review before each execution.

Deviations from the runbook require approval from the Cutover Lead. Any command that fails should trigger the issue escalation process before proceeding. All commands are idempotent where possible, allowing safe retry.

Rollback is the safety net that allows instant reversion to the source system if critical issues are discovered during or after cutover. The rollback window extends until Monday 6 AM (business hours start). After this window, rollback requires extended downtime and data reconciliation. Hypercare Period

Enhanced monitoring and support post-cutover

ğŸ©º 72-Hour Hypercare Sun 2 AM â†’ Wed 2 AM

Hypercare is the 72-hour period immediately following cutover with elevated monitoring, faster SLAs, and dedicated support coverage. During this period, alert thresholds are lowered, response times are accelerated, and the migration team remains on-call for immediate issue resolution. Rollback remains possible during the first 24 hours with minimal data loss.
